## 1. Product Positioning

PlantQuality is the **OEM ↔ Supplier quality management platform** purpose-built for automotive and industrial manufacturers. It is not a generic QMS. It is a **supplier-driven field quality and 8D intelligence platform** where:

- **OEMs** own the quality relationship, define requirements, and drive corrective actions.
- **Suppliers** participate in the workflow — responding to 8Ds, submitting PPAPs, and tracking their own performance — without paying for the platform.
- **Quality intelligence** (AI classification, root cause suggestion, similar issue detection) turns reactive defect management into proactive quality improvement.

The differentiator is the **closed-loop OEM↔Supplier quality workflow** enhanced by AI, not a standalone defect tracker or a generic project management tool.

---

## 2. Plan Philosophy

### Free → Adoption

The Free plan exists to get users into the platform with zero friction. It is not a sustainable operating tier — it is a trial funnel. Free users should experience enough value to understand what PlantQuality does, then hit natural limits that push them toward Pro.

### Pro → Operational Quality Management

Pro is the **primary MRR driver**. It unlocks the full operational loop: defects, field quality, 8D workflows, supplier collaboration, SLA tracking, escalation, and the AI features that accelerate day-to-day quality work (classification, similar issues, category intelligence, quality dashboard).

Every active quality team at an OEM or Tier-1 supplier should be on Pro. This is where the product delivers its core value.

### Enterprise → Intelligence, Control, Integration & Scale

Enterprise is for organizations that need **expert-level AI workflows, organizational control, and integration**. This includes AI 8D Review, Root Cause Suggestion, multi-plant operations, advanced PPAP/IQC/FMEA, SSO, API/Webhook access, and dedicated support.

Enterprise captures high-value accounts with custom pricing and deeper commitments.

### Supplier Portal → Free Participant Model

Supplier users do not pay. The OEM relationship pays for their participation. Suppliers access only records assigned to them — they never see dashboards, AI features, or data beyond their scope. This model maximizes supplier adoption, which in turn increases OEM lock-in and platform stickiness.

## 4. Supplier Portal Strategy

The Supplier Portal must remain **free for supplier-side users** for the following reasons:

1. **OEM pays for the relationship.** The OEM subscription covers all supplier interactions. Charging suppliers would create adoption friction and reduce OEM willingness to onboard suppliers.

2. **Supplier adoption increases OEM lock-in.** Every supplier actively using PlantQuality makes it harder for the OEM to switch platforms. The network effect is a competitive moat.

3. **Suppliers access only assigned records.** A supplier user never sees:
   - Global dashboards or analytics
   - Other suppliers' data
   - AI features (classification, similar issues, root cause, 8D review)
   - Cross-organization quality intelligence
   They see only the 8D reports, defects, and PPAP records where they are an assigned participant.

4. **Supplier AI access is OEM-gated.** If an OEM wants a supplier to benefit from AI-powered features (e.g., AI-assisted 8D response), the OEM's Enterprise plan must explicitly enable it. The supplier does not purchase this independently.

5. **Supplier → OEM upsell path.** If a supplier wants their own PlantQuality instance (to manage their own sub-suppliers), they become a new OEM account — not a free supplier user.

## 9. Implementation Principles for v2.0.0

1. **Central feature gate utility.** Create a single `canAccess(featureKey, session)` utility. All plan checks flow through this. No scattered `if plan === 'PRO'` logic across components.

2. **Backend enforcement is mandatory.** Every Server Action and API route that touches a gated feature must call the feature gate. Frontend gating is for UX only — a user can always bypass client-side checks.

3. **Frontend gating is for UX, not security.** Hide sidebar items, disable buttons, show upgrade CTAs — but never trust the client as the enforcement layer.

4. **Supplier users remain free but restricted by assignment.** Supplier-side access is always scoped to assigned records. The feature gate must check `companyType === 'SUPPLIER'` and restrict accordingly.

5. **Do not build full payment integration in v2.0.0** unless explicitly requested. The focus is on enforcement, limits, and upgrade visibility — not Stripe integration or billing workflows.

6. **Focus on:**
   - Plan enforcement (feature gates + usage limits)
   - Upgrade CTAs (contextual prompts when users hit limits)
   - Admin visibility (plan status, usage, limits in admin UI)

7. **Keep the system configurable.** Plan names, feature keys, and limits should be defined in a central configuration (database or config file), not hardcoded in business logic. This allows non-engineering plan adjustments.

## 11. Out of Scope for v2.0.0

The following are explicitly **not** part of v2.0.0:

- Stripe or payment gateway integration
- Invoice management and billing
- ERP integration (SAP, etc.)
- SSO implementation (SAML/OIDC) — only the gate placeholder
- Advanced billing (prorating, upgrades/downgrades mid-cycle)
- Custom contract negotiation workflow
- Automated plan provisioning upon payment

These may be addressed in v2.1.0+ or as separate initiatives.

## 12. Strategic Summary

| Plan | What It Does |
|------|-------------|
| **Free** | Records quality issues. Gets users in the door with enough value to understand the platform. |
| **Pro** | Manages quality operations. The full operational loop for active quality teams — defects, 8D, suppliers, SLA, AI-assisted classification and intelligence. |
| **Enterprise** | Optimizes quality performance. Expert AI, organizational control, multi-plant scale, integrations, and dedicated support for large OEMs. |
